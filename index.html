Department of Physics, University of Karachi, Pakistan

There is a significant gender gap in physics globally. Many factors contribute to the low number of female physicists, including a lack of access to education, poverty, social and cultural factors and lack of female role models and mentors. This lack of female physicists means we are letting go of a huge potential of scientific progress. To attain UN Sustainable Development Goals, Pakistan needs to empower its female population.

With this motivation, we are arranging the 2nd Physics camp For Girls for girls studying in class 11 and 12, to motivate girls to pursue STEM fields for their bachelor degree. To know about the 1st Physics Camp For Girls, please check out this link .

This camp will include various talks from the distinguished researchers and scholars from the various fields of Physics. There will be various physics demonstration to explain the physics phenomenon in layman's terms. The life of Marie Curie and painting of Cat's Eye Nebula will also be shown in this event. There will be quizzes and as well as career-counseling session in the camp.
